title: "Keychain"
description: "Managing secrets and secure items in PoliteDB"

The **Keychain** in PoliteDB is a dedicated area for managing secrets and other secure items that relate to your database connections.

On macOS, PoliteDB integrates with the **native system Keychain** so that passwords, SSH keys, and other credentials are encrypted and stored by the operating system instead of in plain text files. PoliteDB follows a local‑first, offline‑friendly model and does not send your connection details to any external service.

## Where you use the keychain

From the home screen you can switch between:

- A view of your **connection profiles**.
- A view of your **keychain items**, which lists stored secrets and related metadata.

Both views share the same search and filtering tools, so you can quickly find the profiles or secrets you need.

When you create or edit a connection profile, PoliteDB lets you link fields such as passwords or keys to items stored in the keychain instead of typing or pasting them directly into the profile.

## How connections reference keychain items

Connection profiles can reference secrets stored in the keychain instead of inlining credentials.

In practice this means:

- Sensitive values are stored once in the keychain and reused across profiles where needed.
- Profiles keep a reference to the keychain entry, not the raw secret itself.
- When you open a connection, PoliteDB looks up the relevant secret in the keychain and uses it to authenticate.

This separation keeps the keychain as the **single source of truth** for sensitive values, while connection profiles remain relatively safe to inspect and share.

## Recommended practices

- Store **passwords and API keys** in the keychain, not directly in profiles.
- Use **tags or descriptive names** on keychain items to organize them by environment, team, or purpose.
- Periodically rotate secrets in the keychain and update referencing profiles as needed.
