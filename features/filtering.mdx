Filtering helps you narrow down the data shown in the datagrid so you can focus on the rows that matter most.

You can combine simple conditions and quick searches to answer questions without writing new SQL every time.

## Applying filters

From a datagrid view you can typically:

- Add conditions on one or more columns (for example, equals, contains, greater than).
- Combine multiple filters to refine your result set.
- Temporarily disable or clear filters when you want to see the unfiltered data again.

Filters work on top of the current query or base table, so you can start broad and then narrow down interactively.

## Searching within results

In addition to column‑level filters, you can use a lightweight search:

- Type into a search box to quickly highlight or restrict rows that match your text.
- Use it to find IDs, names, or other distinct values without leaving the current page.

Search is especially useful when you know a specific value you are looking for but do not want to edit the underlying query.

## Best practices

To get the most from filtering:

- Use SQL to define the broad shape of your dataset, then use filters for last‑mile refinement.
- Keep filters simple and composable so they remain easy to understand.
- Clear filters when switching tasks to avoid confusion about why certain rows are hidden.

Filtering is intended as a fast, interactive layer on top of your queries rather than a replacement for precise SQL.
