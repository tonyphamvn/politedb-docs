This guide walks you through installing PoliteDB on macOS and connecting to your first database.

## 1. Check requirements

PoliteDB is a native desktop app that currently targets macOS:

- **Operating system**: macOS Ventura 13.3 or later
- **Architecture**: Apple Silicon (arm64) or Intel (x64)
- **Memory**: 4 GB RAM minimum (8 GB recommended)
- **Disk space**: At least 200 MB free
- **Network**: Internet connection for downloading the app and activating licenses

All database credentials are securely stored in your **macOS Keychain**, and PoliteDB operates in a **local‑first, offline‑friendly** way once installed.

## 2. Download PoliteDB

You can always get the latest builds from the [**Download page**](https://politedb.pages.dev/download)**.**

On the download page, you’ll see macOS installers for:

- **Apple Silicon**: `PoliteDB_version_aarch64.dmg`
- **Intel**: `PoliteDB_version_x64.dmg`

Choose the variant that matches your Mac’s CPU.

## 3. Install on macOS

1. Open the downloaded `.dmg` file.
2. Drag **PoliteDB** into your `Applications` folder.
3. Open **Terminal** and run: `xattr -cr /Applications/PoliteDB.app`
4. Launch **PoliteDB** from Spotlight or the Applications folder.

If macOS warns that the app is from the internet, choose **Open** to continue.

## 4. Create your first connection

When PoliteDB opens:

1. Click **New connection** (or the equivalent action on the home screen).
2. Pick a **database engine**. PoliteDB supports:
   - PostgreSQL
   - MySQL
   - Redis
3. Fill in the connection details (host, port, database, username, and authentication).
4. Save the profile, then click **Open** to launch a workspace tab for that connection.

Your passwords and secrets are stored in the **macOS Keychain**, never in plain text. PoliteDB does not phone home with your connection details.

## 5. Run your first query

Inside a connection workspace:

1. Open a **SQL editor** tab.
2. Type a query (for example, `SELECT 1;` on PostgreSQL or MySQL).
3. Press the run shortcut or click the **Run** button.

You’ll see results inline in the workspace, with history so you can quickly re‑run previous queries.
