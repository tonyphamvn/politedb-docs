title: "Data Grid"
description: "Browse and inspect table data in PoliteDB"
---

The **datagrid** is where PoliteDB displays rows from your tables.

It is built for quick inspection, navigation, and light data exploration rather than heavy reporting.

## Opening the datagrid

You typically reach the datagrid by:

- Selecting a table from the schema navigator.
- Choosing an action such as “open data” or an equivalent from context menus.

PoliteDB opens a new window in the workspace that focuses on that table’s data.

## Reading data

The datagrid shows:

- A header row with column names that match your table schema.
- A scrollable list of rows, with virtualized rendering for large tables.
- Basic indicators for things like nulls or truncated values, depending on the column type.

Use the datagrid for:

- Spot‑checking recent changes.
- Verifying that filters and joins behave as expected.
- Exploring unfamiliar tables before writing more complex SQL.

## Navigation and pagination

Large tables are split into pages so you stay responsive:

- Pagination controls (for example, page numbers or next/previous buttons) let you move through the data.
- You can adjust the page size when you need a broader or narrower view.

Combined with filtering, this helps you focus on the most relevant slice of data without overwhelming the UI.
