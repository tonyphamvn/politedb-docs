---
title: "Safe Mode"
description: "Reduce the risk of accidental destructive changes in PoliteDB"
---

**Safe Mode** is a way to use PoliteDB more cautiously, especially when you are connected to sensitive or production databases.

When Safe Mode is enabled, the app treats potentially destructive operations more carefully and encourages you to double‑check what you are about to do.

## What Safe Mode is for

Safe Mode is designed to:

- Make it harder to accidentally run destructive statements (for example, dropping tables or truncating data).
- Encourage you to review schema changes and bulk updates before they are applied.
- Provide extra confirmation steps when you are performing high‑impact actions.

It is especially useful when:

- Working against production or business‑critical databases.
- Pairing or reviewing work where multiple people may be running queries from the same environment.
- Exploring unfamiliar schemas where the impact of a change is not yet clear.

## Typical behavior

In Safe Mode, you can expect PoliteDB to:

- Highlight or clearly label destructive actions in menus and dialogs.
- Ask for explicit confirmation before running operations that:
  - Drop or truncate tables.
  - Rewrite large portions of data.
  - Apply structural changes to schemas.
- Encourage you to review generated SQL or summaries of changes before they are executed.

Safe Mode is not about blocking every write; it is about slowing you down slightly for operations that are hard or impossible to undo.

## Enabling and disabling Safe Mode

Safe Mode is typically **off by default** so you can work quickly in development and test environments.

You can turn it on when you need extra protection, for example:

- Before connecting to a production database.
- At the start of a session where you know you will be reviewing or auditing changes.

Once enabled, Safe Mode applies across the relevant parts of the app until you explicitly turn it off again.

## Best practices

To get the most value from Safe Mode:

- Treat it as a standard part of your workflow for production and regulated environments.
- Combine it with backups and query history so you can verify and, when necessary, recover from changes.
- Leave it off in ephemeral or local environments where you are intentionally moving fast and the cost of mistakes is low.

Safe Mode is one layer in a broader safety story that also includes permissions, backups, and careful review of generated SQL.
